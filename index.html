ABOUT ME

Engineer & Maker

I'm a 2nd year CS student at GITAM Hyderabad who enjoys building real systems—both hardware and software.

My interests are more on the low-level side of tech: firmware, embedded systems, operating systems, memory, binaries, and cybersecurity. I'm especially curious about how things work underneath the surface and enjoy exploring system internals more than traditional frontend development.

Currently, I'm learning Unity, game development, game theory, and experimenting with interactive design through projects like my portfolio website.

I also enjoy working with physical systems—3D printing, creative fabrication, mechanical engineering concepts, and turning ideas into working prototypes. I'm a passionate 3D printing enthusiast and enjoy understanding how machines are designed and built.

I'm a huge supporter of open source software and prefer community-driven tools over closed ecosystems. Recently, I repurposed my old TV to run an open-source YouTube frontend, just because I love giving old hardware new life.

Fun fact: I've procrastinated a lot and don't always stay consistent with projects or learning paths. I started learning Rust, but after two weeks I lost track of it due to inconsistency. I also tend to leave many projects unfinished in my "graveyard" folder instead of returning to them—but I'm working on becoming more disciplined and finishing what I start.
